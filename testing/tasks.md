# Critical User Tasks — Smart Onboarding

**Project:** HiveMind × Ravensbourne – UX Product Discovery Sprint
**Focus Area:** Smart Onboarding
**Author:** Team 6
**Last Updated:** 2026-04-11

---

## Overview

These 3 tasks define exactly what we will design and test in Weeks 3–4. Each task is tied directly to a Week 1 hypothesis and a specific pain point heard in interviews.

---

## Task 1 — Complete the Day 1 checklist and know what to do next

**User goal:**
> A new starter opens the platform on their first day and completes their Day 1 checklist, then clearly understands what their next step is.

**Linked hypothesis:** H1 — A structured, role-specific onboarding journey reduces overwhelm

**Why this task matters:**
Every participant described their first days as unstructured and confusing. P1 said "I still had to figure out many things on my own." P2 and P3 said onboarding felt like "box-ticking" with no clear daily structure. This task tests whether the guided checklist actually removes that confusion.

**Success criteria:**
- User can find and open the Day 1 checklist without help
- User can mark tasks as complete
- User can identify their next task without being prompted
- Completion time: ≤ 2 minutes

---

## Task 2 — Check onboarding progress and identify the next milestone

**User goal:**
> A new starter at the end of Week 1 checks how far through their onboarding they are, and can clearly state what their next milestone is.

**Linked hypothesis:** H4 — A dashboard showing progress and tasks helps employees feel on track

**Why this task matters:**
Participants described relying on personal notes, bookmarks, and memory — all of which were described as "messy" and "hard to manage." P3 said "tasks and deadlines are the most important thing." D (Healthcare) said "I would love to have it display my tasks and progress." This task tests whether the progress view actually gives a clear sense of where the user stands.

**Success criteria:**
- User can locate the progress overview without help
- User can correctly state their current week and how many tasks remain
- User can identify the next upcoming milestone
- Completion time: ≤ 60 seconds

---

## Task 3 — Find who to contact when stuck on a role-specific question

**User goal:**
> A new starter has a question they can't answer themselves. They use the platform to find the right person to ask — without messaging multiple people or guessing.

**Linked hypothesis:** H5 — Making ownership visible during onboarding helps new starters get help faster

**Why this task matters:**
P1 said "Not always" when asked if they knew who to ask. P2 said "I didn't know who was responsible for what" — and that this caused delays and stress. P3 echoed the same. This task tests whether the Support & Contacts section genuinely removes the guesswork of who to approach.

**Success criteria:**
- User can navigate to the Support & Contacts section without help
- User can identify the correct contact for their question (Manager, Buddy, HR, or IT)
- User does not backtrack more than once
- Completion time: ≤ 45 seconds

## How These Will Be Tested (Week 4)

- Each task will be given to 3–5 professional participants
- Participants will think aloud while completing each task in the Figma prototype
- For each task we record: success / failure, time taken, and a 1–5 ease rating
- Results feed into `/delivery/validation-report.md`
