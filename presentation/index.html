01 — The Problem

Onboarding is broken
for most new starters

Every person we interviewed described their first days as chaotic, confusing, and isolating.

📋No clear structure or "what to do next" on Day 1
🗂️Information scattered across multiple tools — Drive, Teams, email, Slack
❓New starters don't know who to ask for help or who owns what
📝People rely on messy personal notes and memory — unreliable at scale
🤖AI tools are used but not trusted without source references

02 — Research

Key findings

1

Generic, not role-specific

Onboarding felt like box-ticking — no personalisation to role, department, or seniority.

2

No single source of truth

Documents saved in wrong folders, outdated versions, no way to verify correctness.

3

Unclear ownership

New starters didn't know who to ask — causing delays, stress, and workarounds.

4

Manual tracking is failing

Everyone relied on personal notes and bookmarks — "messy and hard to manage."

5

AI trust requires transparency

Participants use AI but demand source references. No citation = no trust.

6

No visible progress

New starters couldn't tell how far through onboarding they were or what came next.

02 — Research

Our persona:
Christoph

👤

New Professional Starter

0–2 weeks into a new role. Joining a company with existing tools, processes, and people they don't know yet.

Day 1–14 Professional role 1+ year experience
GOAL

Feel confident, productive, and connected from day one — without having to figure everything out alone.

FRUSTRATIONS
  • No clear structure on Day 1
  • Can't find information when needed
  • Doesn't know who to ask
  • No sense of progress or direction
NEEDS
  • Role-specific daily guidance
  • One place for all key information
  • Clear contact ownership
  • Visible progress milestones
03 — Hypotheses

5 hypotheses.
All grounded in evidence.

H1

A structured, role-specific journey reduces overwhelm and boosts early productivity

All 5 participants described chaotic, unstructured first days with no clear daily plan or role-specific guidance.
H2

A centralised Knowledge Hub reduces time spent searching for information

"I spent more time searching for things than actually doing my job." — P2 (Marketing)
H3

An AI Assistant that shows its sources will increase employee trust in AI answers

"If the response seems unclear or possibly incorrect, I lose trust immediately." — P2
H4

A progress dashboard with tasks and deadlines helps new starters feel on track

"I would love to have it display my tasks and progress." — D (Healthcare)
H5

Making ownership visible during onboarding helps new starters get help faster

"I didn't know who was responsible for what." — P3
04 — Design

Information Architecture

5 sections — each mapped directly to a hypothesis and interview pain point

1 · Welcome & Setup → H1

Personalised entry point. Role-specific Day 1 guidance. Profile and preferences setup.

2 · My Onboarding Journey → H1, H4

Week-by-week roadmap. Milestone tracker. Unlocking content progressively over time.

3 · Guided Next Steps → H4

Daily task checklist. Deadline visibility. Progress banner with next recommended action.

4 · Support & Contacts → H5

Contact ownership by role (Manager, Buddy, HR, IT). FAQ. Direct message form.

5 · Resources → H2

Centralised knowledge base. Role-filtered documents. Single source of truth.

04 — Design

3 critical user tasks

TASK 1 · H1

Complete the Day 1 checklist and know what to do next

A new starter opens the platform on their first day, completes the checklist, and clearly identifies their next step — without help.

≤ 2 minutes
TASK 2 · H4

Check onboarding progress and identify the next milestone

At the end of Week 1, the new starter checks how far through their onboarding they are and can state their next milestone.

≤ 60 seconds
TASK 3 · H5

Find who to contact when stuck on a role-specific question

A new starter has a question they can't answer alone. They find the right person to ask — without guessing or messaging multiple people.

≤ 45 seconds

Each task will be tested with 3–5 professional participants in Week 4. Participants think aloud while completing each task in the Figma prototype. We record: success / failure · time taken · ease rating (1–5).
