# Hypotheses

> Based on Week 1 interviews with 5 working professionals across retail, digital marketing, hospitality, and healthcare industries.

---

## Pain Points Identified

Across all 5 interviews, the following recurring themes emerged:

- **Unstructured onboarding** — participants felt overwhelmed on their first days with no clear plan or "what to do next" guidance
- **Information scattered across multiple tools** — people had to check Google Drive, ask colleagues, search notes, and still sometimes couldn't find what they needed
- **No single source of truth** — documents saved in wrong folders, outdated versions, no way to verify if info is correct
- **Not knowing who to ask** — unclear ownership of knowledge and responsibilities
- **Manual, messy personal tracking** — people rely on personal notes, bookmarks, and memory which are unreliable and hard to scale
- **Conditional AI trust** — participants use AI tools (ChatGPT) but don't fully trust them without source references or verification
- **Privacy concerns around AI** — participants were cautious about sharing internal or personal data with AI tools

---

## Hypotheses

**H1: We believe that a structured, role-specific onboarding journey will help new starters feel less lost and more productive in their first weeks, because every participant described their first days as confusing and overwhelming, with no clear plan or daily structure.**

> Evidence: P1 (Tesco) said "I still had to figure out many things on my own." P2 and P3 (Marketing Executives) both said onboarding felt like "box-ticking" with no role-specific guidance. Yena (Event Manager) described her first solo event as "super stressed" and "out of control" due to lack of preparation support.

---

**H2: We believe that a centralised Knowledge Hub will reduce the time employees spend searching for information, because participants consistently reported having to check multiple places and still failing to find what they needed.**

> Evidence: P2 (Marketing) said "I spent more time searching for things than actually doing my job." P3 said "It takes a lot of time and sometimes I still can't find the right thing." P1 (Tesco) described needing to check the till system, ask colleagues, and sometimes a manager — all for a single answer. Yena said she has to "search everywhere" and relies on personal notes that become unmanageable.

---

**H3: We believe that an AI Assistant that clearly shows its sources will significantly increase employee trust in AI-generated answers, because participants said they would trust AI more if it referenced where information came from, and distrusted it when answers felt unclear or unverifiable.**

> Evidence: P2 said "I'd trust it more if it could pull accurate information from multiple company sources quickly — if the response seems unclear or possibly incorrect, I lose trust immediately." P3 agreed: "If the answer is unclear or probably wrong, then I don't trust it." D (Healthcare) said they always follow up AI answers with their own guidelines and don't fully depend on it.

---

**H4: We believe that a dashboard showing daily tasks, key deadlines, and important links will help employees feel on track and in control, because participants said they currently rely on scattered personal notes, bookmarks, and memory — all of which are unreliable.**

> Evidence: P2 and P3 both said they use personal notes and bookmarks that "get messy and hard to manage." P1 said they rely on memory which "isn't always reliable." When asked what a home screen should show, P3 said "tasks and deadlines are the most important." D (Healthcare) said "I would love to have it display my tasks and progress."

---

**H5: We believe that clearly defining who is responsible for what — and making that visible during onboarding — will reduce confusion and help new starters get help faster, because multiple participants said they didn't know who to ask when they needed help.**

> Evidence: P1 said "Not always" when asked if they knew who to ask. P3 said "I didn't know who was responsible for what." P2 said the same issue caused delays and stress. Only D (Healthcare) felt supported, crediting a hands-on team — not a system — as the reason.
