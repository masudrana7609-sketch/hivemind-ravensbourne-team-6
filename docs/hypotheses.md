# Hypotheses — Smart Onboarding

## Core Hypotheses

### H1 — Personalised onboarding reduces time-to-productivity
**We believe** that tailoring the onboarding journey to each new hire's role, experience level, and learning style  
**will result in** faster time to first meaningful contribution  
**we will know this is true when** average time-to-productivity drops by ≥ 20% compared to the current baseline.

---

### H2 — Information overload is the primary failure point in current onboarding
**We believe** that new hires are overwhelmed by receiving too much information at once  
**will result in** low retention of key knowledge and reduced confidence in the first 30 days  
**we will know this is true when** user research interviews and surveys reveal information overload as a top-3 pain point in ≥ 60% of participants.

---

### H3 — Just-in-time knowledge delivery improves retention
**We believe** that surfacing the right information at the moment it is needed (rather than day one)  
**will result in** higher task completion rates and lower repetitive questions to managers  
**we will know this is true when** we see a measurable reduction in repeated queries and support tickets from new hires in the first 90 days.

---

### H4 — Social connection accelerates onboarding success
**We believe** that facilitating early connections between new hires and relevant colleagues (buddies, team members, cross-functional peers)  
**will result in** higher engagement scores and lower 90-day attrition  
**we will know this is true when** new hires with facilitated connections report higher belonging scores (≥ 15% uplift) vs. control group.

---

### H5 — Progress visibility motivates new hires
**We believe** that showing new hires a clear onboarding roadmap with visible milestones  
**will result in** increased completion of onboarding tasks and reduced anxiety  
**we will know this is true when** onboarding task completion rate exceeds 85% within the first 30 days.

---

## Assumptions to Validate

| # | Assumption | Risk Level | Validation Method |
|---|------------|------------|-------------------|
| A1 | New hires want a personalised experience, not a one-size-fits-all path | High | User interviews, survey |
| A2 | Managers are a bottleneck in the current process | Medium | Manager interviews, time-tracking data |
| A3 | Company has sufficient data to personalise at scale | High | Technical discovery spike |
| A4 | New hires are comfortable with digital-first onboarding tools | Low | Usability testing |
| A5 | HR/People team will support and maintain the smart system | Medium | Stakeholder interviews |
