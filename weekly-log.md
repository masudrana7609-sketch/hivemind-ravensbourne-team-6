- No clear structure or "what to do next" on Day 1
- Onboarding felt generic — not role-specific
- Information scattered across multiple tools
- Did not know who to ask or who owned what
- Relied on personal notes and memory (messy and unreliable)
- AI tools used but not trusted without source references

## Week 2 — Information Architecture & Wireframing

**Period:** w/c 6 April 2026
**Goal:** Build IA for Smart Onboarding and create low-fidelity wireframes for 3 critical user tasks.

### What was done

| Activity | Who | Output |
|---|---|---|
| Re-read Week 1 hypotheses and interview evidence | Group Lead | Confirmed H1, H4, H5 as priority drivers for IA |
| Designed Information Architecture (5-section sitemap) | Group Lead + IA Designer | `docs/ia.md` |

### Screens built (Week 2 wireframes)

| Screen | Purpose | Task |
|---|---|---|
| W1 – Welcome Screen | Personalised entry point for new starter | Task 1 |
| W2 – Day 1 Checklist | Guided checklist with progress banner and next step | Task 1 |
| W3 – Task Completed | Confirmation state + Day 1 complete card | Task 1 |
| W4 – Progress Overview | Stats, progress bar, weekly breakdown, milestones | Task 2 |
| W5 – Support & Contacts | Contact cards by role + FAQ section | Task 3 |
| W6 – Contact Detail | Expanded contact view + ask a question form | Task 3 |

### Key decisions made
- 5-section IA based directly on H1, H2, H4, H5
- Persistent left sidebar navigation (matches shadcn enterprise patterns)
- 3 critical user tasks selected (T1, T2, T3)
- All wireframes grey-box only — high-fidelity deferred to Week 3

### Time spent (estimated)
- IA design and documentation: ~2 hours
- Task definition: ~1 hour
- Wireframing in Figma (6 screens): ~3 hours
- GitHub documentation: ~1 hour
