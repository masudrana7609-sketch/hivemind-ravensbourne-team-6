**Interviewer:** Toriqul\
**Interviewee:** Unknown Speaker\
**Role:** Marketing Executive\
**Industry:** Digital Marketing\
**Duration:** 35 Minutes

**Introduction**

This interview was conducted as part of a study for Ravensbourne
University, focusing on employee onboarding experiences and how
individuals find and manage information in the workplace.

**Section A: Role & Company Context**

**Toriqul:**\
Hi, thank you so much for joining me today. We're conducting a study for
Ravensbourne about onboarding and how employees find information at
work. Is it okay if I record this?

**Unknown Speaker:**\
Yes, that's absolutely fine.

**Toriqul:**\
Great. To start, could you tell me a bit about your current job and the
company you work for?

**Unknown Speaker:**\
I'm a Marketing Executive in the digital marketing industry. My
day-to-day work involves planning campaigns and managing social media.
The company has around 200 employees.

**Section B: The Onboarding Experience**

**Toriqul:**\
Think back to when you first started. What did those first few days look
like?

**Unknown Speaker:**\
Honestly, it was a bit confusing and awkward. I was the "new person,"
and although everyone was friendly, I didn't really know where to begin.

**Toriqul:**\
Why did it feel awkward? Can you explain a bit more?

**Unknown Speaker:**\
There was a lot to learn but no clear structure or guidance. In those
first two weeks, I really needed clarity on my daily tasks and how the
company actually operated. I also didn't know where important files were
stored.

**Toriqul:**\
What was the most difficult part of those first two weeks?

**Unknown Speaker:**\
Definitely finding information. It felt like I spent more time searching
for things than actually doing my job.

**Toriqul:**\
Did the onboarding feel tailored to your role, or more like a general
checklist?

**Unknown Speaker:**\
Some parts were useful, but a lot of it felt like box-ticking---just
going through standard steps without really helping me in my specific
role.

**Section C: Information Retrieval & Barriers**

**Toriqul:**\
When you have a question now, where do you usually look first?

**Unknown Speaker:**\
I usually check Google Drive first, or sometimes I just ask a team
member if I can't find what I need.

**Toriqul:**\
How often do you end up checking more than one place?

**Unknown Speaker:**\
Almost every time. It's quite frustrating because it wastes a lot of
time. Sometimes I search multiple places and still can't find the right
document.

**Toriqul:**\
Can you share a recent example of this?

**Unknown Speaker:**\
Yes, just last week I needed a client document. It wasn't in the correct
folder---it had been saved in the wrong place. I ended up asking three
different people just to locate one file.

**Toriqul:**\
That sounds frustrating. How do you try to stay organised personally?

**Unknown Speaker:**\
I use browser bookmarks and personal notes, but it gets messy over time.
Once there's too much information, it becomes hard to manage.

**Section D: AI and Trust in the Workplace**

**Toriqul:**\
Let's talk about AI. Have you used tools like ChatGPT or Copilot to help
with these challenges?

**Unknown Speaker:**\
Yes, I use ChatGPT quite a lot. It's very fast, which I like, but the
accuracy isn't always reliable.

**Toriqul:**\
What would make you trust an AI tool more in a work environment?

**Unknown Speaker:**\
I'd trust it more if it could pull accurate information from multiple
company sources quickly. But if the response seems unclear or possibly
incorrect, I lose trust immediately.

**Toriqul:**\
Are there situations where AI should not provide answers?

**Unknown Speaker:**\
Definitely. For sensitive or critical questions, the AI should simply
direct you to ask a human instead.

**Section E: Wrap-Up & Solutions**

**Toriqul:**\
To wrap up, what are the two biggest challenges you face today?

**Unknown Speaker:**\
First is finding information, and second is the lack of a clear
onboarding plan.

**Toriqul:**\
What impact do these issues have on your work?

**Unknown Speaker:**\
They cause a lot of stress and lead to delays in projects.

**Toriqul:**\
If you could improve one thing to make starting a job easier, what would
it be?

**Unknown Speaker:**\
A single platform where everything---tasks, links, and documents---is
organised in one place would make a huge difference.

**Toriqul:**\
Thank you so much for your time and for sharing your insights.

**Unknown Speaker:**\
You're welcome!
