# Interview Transcript (Week 1 Research)

**Interviewer:** Nojus
**Participant:** P06 (Project Coordinator)
**Industry:** Architecture & Construction
**Duration:** ~30 minutes

---

**Nojus:** Hi there, thank you for joining this session. As mentioned, this is for my university research project regarding workplace onboarding and information management. Is it okay if I record this for my notes?

**P06:** Yes, that's fine. No problem.

**Nojus:** Great. Your identity will be kept anonymous. To start, could you describe your current role and how long you've been with the firm?

**P06:** I'm a Project Coordinator. I've been here about seven months. My job is basically the "middle man" — I coordinate between the architects' designs and the site managers who are actually building the structures.

**Nojus:** Think back to your first week on the job. What was that experience like for you?

**P06:** Honestly? It was a bit of a "sink or swim" situation. Everyone was incredibly busy because we had two major projects launching. I was handed a laptop and told to "look through the server" to get up to speed.

**Nojus:** Did you feel lost at any point?

**P06:** Constantly. In those first two weeks, I really needed to know the specific safety protocols for each site and who the main points of contact were for the contractors. But there was no "map." I spent more time trying to find the right person to ask than actually doing the work.

**Nojus:** Did the company provide any formal training or onboarding?

**P06:** It felt very "box-ticking." I had to watch some generic safety videos and sign a handbook, but it didn't help me understand how our specific office managed files or tracked project changes. It wasn't tailored to my role at all.

**Nojus:** When you have a technical question or need a specific document now, where do you go first?

**P06:** I usually start with our internal Shared Drive, but it's a total graveyard of old versions. If I can't find it in five minutes, I'll message a colleague on Teams or check my email history.

**Nojus:** How often are you checking more than one place for a single answer?

**P06:** Almost every single time. It's incredibly frustrating. You find a blueprint in the "Final" folder, but then you find a "REVISED_FINAL" version in a random email thread from three weeks ago. You never know which one is the actual "truth."

**Nojus:** Can you give me a recent example of when this caused a problem?

**P06:** Just last Tuesday. I sent a material order to a site based on a spreadsheet I found on the drive. Turns out, the site manager had updated a different spreadsheet on his own laptop and forgot to upload it. We ordered the wrong timber. It caused a two-day delay.

**Nojus:** How does that affect your stress levels?

**P06:** It's a vicious circle. The more time I spend hunting for info, the further behind I get, which makes me more stressed, which leads to more mistakes. It's exhausting.

**Nojus:** Have you experimented with any AI tools, like ChatGPT, to help with these tasks?

**P06:** I use it to help summarize long meeting minutes or to draft professional emails when I'm in a rush. It's fast, which is the main draw.

**Nojus:** What would make you trust an AI tool to give you information from your company documents?

**P06:** It would have to cite its sources. If it tells me "The safety deadline is Friday," I want to see the link to the original document so I can verify it. If it just gives me a "hallucination," I lose trust immediately.

**Nojus:** Are there any areas where you think AI should not be used?

**P06:** Anything involving safety risks or sensitive personnel issues. You can't replace a human's "gut feeling" or empathy with an algorithm when things get critical.

**Nojus:** To wrap up, what are the two biggest challenges you face in your day-to-day work?

**P06:** First is the fragmented information — files being scattered everywhere. Second is the lack of a structured plan for when new projects or people start.

**Nojus:** If you could change one thing to make your job easier, what would it be?

**P06:** A "Single Source of Truth." One centralised dashboard where I can see my tasks, the latest document versions, and the key contacts in one place. No more jumping between five different apps.

**Nojus:** Thank you so much for your time. This has been very helpful.

**P06:** You're very welcome. Good luck with the research!
