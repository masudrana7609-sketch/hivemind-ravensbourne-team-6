Interview Transcript (Week 1 Research)

Interviewer: Arnob

Participant: P1 (Tesco Sales Assistant)

Duration: \~35 minutes

Introduction & Consent

Arnob: Hi, thanks for taking the time to speak with me today. This
interview is part of my university

research project.

Arnob: I'll be asking about your experience at work, especially around
how you find information,

onboarding, and daily tasks.

Arnob: The interview will take around 30 to 40 minutes. I'll be
recording this only for transcription

and academic purposes.

Arnob: Your identity will remain anonymous, and I'll refer to you as
Participant 1.

Arnob: Is that okay with you?

P1: Yes, that's fine.

Arnob: Great, thank you. We can begin.

Arnob: To start, can you describe your role at Tesco and how long you've
been working there?

P1: I work as a sales assistant, mainly on the shop floor and sometimes
at the tills. I've been

working there for about a year.

Arnob: Can you describe your first few days when you started?

P1: The first few days were quite overwhelming. There was a lot to
learn, especially the store layout

and different responsibilities.

Arnob: What kind of onboarding or training did you receive?

P1: I mostly shadowed other employees. It helped, but it wasn't very
structured.

Arnob: In your first two weeks, what were the main things you needed to
learn?

P1: Using the till system, understanding product locations, and how to
deal with customers.

Arnob: What was the most challenging part during that time?

P1: Remembering product locations and responding quickly to customers.

Arnob: Did you feel the onboarding process was effective?

P1: Partly, but I still had to figure out many things on my own.

Arnob: When you need information during your shift, where do you usually
go?

P1: I usually ask colleagues or a manager, and sometimes check the
system.

Arnob: Do you often need to check multiple sources?

P1: Yes, quite often.

Arnob: Can you give an example where finding information was difficult?

P1: When customers ask about stock availability, it can take time to
confirm.

Arnob: How does that affect your work?

P1: It can slow things down and make it stressful during busy times.

Arnob: How do you keep track of important information?

P1: Mostly through memory or sometimes notes on my phone.

Arnob: What are the limitations of that?

P1: It's not always reliable and easy to forget things.

Arnob: If you had a single app or system, what would you want in it?

P1: Product locations, stock information, shift details, and quick
guides.

Arnob: What should be quickest to access?

P1: Product locations and stock levels.

Arnob: Thinking back, did you know what to do each day in your first
week?

P1: Not really, I often had to ask others.

Arnob: What would have improved that experience?

P1: A clearer structure or guide for new starters.

Arnob: Did you always know who to ask for help?

P1: Not always.

Arnob: What systems do you currently use?

P1: Mainly the till system and some internal tools.

Arnob: What improvements would you suggest?

P1: Make them simpler and more user-friendly.

Arnob: Would you trust AI tools at work?

P1: For basic tasks, yes, but I would still verify important
information.

Arnob: When should AI not be used?

P1: For serious customer issues or complaints.

Arnob: What are the biggest challenges in your role?

P1: Finding information quickly and handling busy periods.

Arnob: What impact do these challenges have?

P1: They increase stress and slow down work.

Arnob: If you could change one thing, what would it be?

P1: Having all information in one easy-to-access system.

Arnob: Thank you, that's all my questions.

P1: You're welcome.
